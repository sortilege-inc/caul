Dramatis Persona

Brathis

Brathis is a goddess associated with shrines and significant artifacts, embodying the concept of "vessels." This aspect intrigues Heyou, who seeks to understand his own nature. Illustrations in various texts depict shrines dedicated to Brathis, emphasizing her importance in the world's lore.

Heyou experiences a profound reaction upon encountering a mandala illustration related to Brathis, temporarily losing control of his limbs until Tamber intervenes. This incident leaves him agitated and eager to learn more about the mandala and its significance. Discussions around Brathis and the mandala during a community dinner illustrate the exploration of her role in Heyou's journey.

Recently, it has come to light that Brathis is also the goddess of feral survival and the loss of personhood. She is represented by the iconography of three fallen stalactites forming a triangle, resembling the altar formations in the World Below. Her influence is tied to the haunting sensations of Splendor magic, signifying a connection to primal forces. She is one of the eight gods involved in a plan to draw divine entities into the World Below to prevent the ruin of the upper world, alongside Ulmira, Tlellic, Kreth, Mollthain, Caranthalis, Ossa, and God-King Otheidas.

Sylvie recognizes Brathis's symbol while examining a cache room filled with various items, including crates, statues, and weapons. This symbol evokes memories of altars dedicated to Brathis, leading her to reflect on themes of survival and transformation. She understands Brathis's influence over those who have lost their humanity in pursuit of survival or power.

Additionally, Ygva, a powerful witch, intends to gather remnants and totems of the gods, including Brathis, to bind them with titanic aid. The remnants of the gods are crucial for restoring life and balance in the world, highlighting the necessity of drawing Brathis back to address the deteriorating conditions impacting both the upper and inner worlds.
