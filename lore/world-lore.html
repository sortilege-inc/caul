REGION: ULMIRA'S STEP

              • A remote Ridgeborne settlement perched high in the mountains of the Halcyon Domain.
              • Said to be the *first step* of a forgotten pilgrimage once walked in honor of the departed god Ulmira.
              • The lower portions of massive carved stone steps are still visible; upper steps are lost beneath avalanche rubble.
              • A Sacred Pyre burns high in an exposed basalt cistern at the town's center, maintained as a beacon of safety and community.

THE KURAEL

              • The people of Ulmira’s Step call themselves Kurael, meaning “those who endure upon the mountains.”
              • Their primary dialect is Lammat, shaped by the wind and stone of their mountain homes.
              • An older ritual language, Kurethik, is still preserved and used in ceremonies. It predates the Apostasy.
              • Lammat lacks the consonants "p" and "b" entirely.

ANCESTRY TERMS

              • Shargan: The Kurael name for themselves and other mountain-born Giants. Adults typically range from 5 to 10 feet tall, most often 7–9 feet.
              • Lenvar: Humans; means "tide-chasers," referencing their fleeting, migratory nature.
              • Drakenn: Dwarves; means "fire-sleepers," referencing their subterranean life and connection to heat and stone.

THE ASKAVIR

              • A massive raptor-like being, seen in silhouette against the clouds or moon
              • Appears rarely—at most once per month.
              • Its name, Askavir, comes from the Lammat dialect.
              • Rumored to have been the familiar of the Veiled God Tlellic
              • Its presence inspires awe and fear but it rarely attacks the Step directly.

THE TITAN SKELETON

              • At the mountain's base, just outside of Ulmira's Step, near the road to Drosven’s Gate, lies the remains of a massive titan clad in ancient metal armor.
              • The skeleton is semi-buried, with teeth the size of a grown human and bones large enough to walk through.
              • It has been dead since at least the Apostasy; rumors abound.
      • History & Setting
          • ~100 years ago, the gods left.
          • Around the same time, settlements began to craft sacred pyres, lit by blessed branches.
          • Ever since, umbral shadow has threatened all areas not graced by sacred pyres, and horrifying monsters have lurked in the dark.
          • Death has a risk of blighting the soul, sometimes causing the recently deceased to pose a threat to their former community.
          • Region was formerly known as the Halcyon Domain. No one now refers to it as such.
          • There is/was a large city called Amber Reach, on the western side of the mountains.
          • Recently: something caused the pyre in Drosven’s Gate to spontaneously go out. The cause is unknown, as is whether there is precedent.
          • Age of Titans
              • Predates known history, but there are features that demonstrate that titans must once have been present in the Halcyon Domain.

  • Lore Known to Heyou
      • Dimmers

Dimmers is a catch-all term for multiple, semi-overlapping groups that are basically stealing the copper wire from the ruins of the Halcyon Domain. Some are taking scarce blessed branches to light their waves into temple and palace ruins for delving; others are more interested in getting a leg up on other survivors.

You've only had direct interactions with two subsets: the group that is interested in reactivating the old stone clanks, and the group that is focused on recycling found and stolen materials.
