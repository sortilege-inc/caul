The Snuffing of the Pyre of Drosven’s Gate

Session 01·2025-06-19

Summary

In the heart of Drosven’s Gate, Sylvie Cerridwen, Heyou, Jamal, and Draz gather at the Sacred Pyre, a flickering beacon of magic and life. The air’s thick with tension, and then it happens—the pyre snuffs out, plunging the plaza into darkness.

  • A massive chitinous creature erupts from the cracked earth of the Idol Hollows, a dire threat looming over the town.
  • Heyou, quick on his feet, darts into the shadows, feeling the sting of acid graze him.
  • Jamal, the dwarf Slayer, stands firm, swinging his warhammer and striking the beast, sending ripples of darkness through the plaza.
  • Two gelatinous shapes ooze forth; one menaces Sylvie, who retaliates with a concussive spell, slamming it against a wall but failing to destroy it.
  • Another jelly surges toward Jamal; he intercepts it with a thunderous blow, exploding it in a burst of bile that eats away at his armor and skin.
  • Draz scales the extinguished pyre, firing an arrow that pierces one jelly’s nucleus, halting its regeneration.
  • Heyou circles the battlefield, dispatching the remaining jelly halves with his daggers, ensuring they can't come back.
  • The bus-sized chitinous creature repeatedly burrows away and resurfaces; it is finally destroyed when Jamal and Heyou tag-team it, bursting it apart so it can no longer retreat underground. The group regroups over the cold embers of the pyre.
  • Jamal asks about relighting the pyre; Sylvie reveals they need a Blessed Branch to restore its magic.
  • Draz warns that the defeated creatures won’t stay dead unless burned, stressing caution.
  • Recalling his own road here — from Vireth Hollow in the southeast, up through Larrow — Draz warns what a dead pyre means: Larrow was once a bustling hub town, but when its pyre went out and could not be relit it became a ghost-town ruin, some fleeing and some staying to die. Drosven’s Gate faces the same fate.
  • Sylvie knows of two rumored sources of a Blessed Branch: high in the mountains beyond Ulmira’s Step — near her native Stoneheart Peaks, where the giants are said to keep a closely-guarded stash — or among the fairies of Thistleglass. She steers the party toward the mountains, chiefly because she distrusts the fairies. The party agrees to secure the town, then departs.

The townsfolk scatter, fear gripping them, while we gather provisions from abandoned stalls and ale reserves (Heyou also lifts a few extra handfuls of gold from the bar's tips). It is autumn, turning toward winter; we set out under the years-old permanent high cloud-cover on the four-to-nine-day climb toward the mountains, into constant cold, dry wind.

  • The atmosphere grows eerily quiet, birds vanish, and Sylvie's demeanor darkens.
  • A vast shadow sweeps overhead — the Askavir, a titan-raptor and the only one of its kind, an order of magnitude larger than any ordinary mountain bird. Sylvie recognizes it only by analogy: in the Stoneheart Peaks, a select order of her people — the riders of the Eyrie — were marked as children to live higher up and ride the great mountain birds, carrying messages across the umbral region and reaching places otherwise cut off. The Askavir is no bird of the Eyrie — it is something far greater and singular — but she reasons about it as she would one of their mounts, and reads that this one is only patrolling, not hunting.
  • Draz veers off the road and lands in a grove of aware, moving trees; roots ensnare him, and another tree seizes Heyou as he tries to sneak past.
  • An articulate tree speaks for the grove, demanding penitence for the trespass. Its brethren are blighted — they still move, but their minds are stultified and their personhood reduced, caught between life and death. Sylvie heals them by drawing on the "heart of the earth" that once empowered her — a slow heartbeat stirring within — and the trees report their roots feel strengthened, releasing Draz and Heyou.
  • From the tree, Sylvie learns what a Blessed Branch truly is: not a crafted thing, but a natural rarity — small, hardy scrub-trees in the high crags now and then grow a single branch of distinct bright-blue flowers (sometimes streaked crimson) instead of the usual white and orange, like a four-leaf clover, found only at higher altitudes.
  • Earlier, drawing a tarot card on the road ahead, Sylvie turns up the Moon reversed — she always draws the Moon, and its reversal unsettles her as she is pulled back toward the peaks where she lost her power and has never returned.
  • To lighten the mood, Heyou recounts Ugg and Grug, two giant brothers — one near ten and a half feet, the other a mere nine — who were muscle for a rival syndicate. Between them they have four eyes (one has one, the other three); Heyou could cheat Ugg blind at cards but never got anything past Grug. (Heyou's own syndicate was based out of the cliff-carved port of Salthewn.)

We spot the colossal remains of a long-dead titan embedded in a cliff, its massive teeth looming over us. We pass remnants of ancient armor, rusted and half-buried, until we crest a ridge and behold the stone stairs of Ulmira’s Step, with the broken palisades of the town in the distance.

Cost: A cold pyre, wounded bodies, and the weight of fear pressing down on us. Loose ends: The Blessed Branch still out there, the townsfolk’s fate uncertain, and the looming shadow of that raptor.
