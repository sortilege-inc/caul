Session

Void Moon

Session 07·2025-10-14

Summary

The party rolled into the Witch’s Mountainside Camp, looking for shelter and answers after the chaos that had unfolded. Sylvie was a mess, heartless and oozing thick black fluid. Heyou took a look, noting her strange condition. Draz caught a whiff of something starchy, adding to the oddity of the scene. We decided to rest up and plot our next move.

  • Heyou rummaged through the witch’s supplies, finding dried berries and witchcraft ingredients but no maps or notes.
  • We snagged three minor health potions and three stamina potions, splitting them among ourselves. Draz grabbed a satchel of healing leaves and fungi.
  • Heyou stumbled upon a sealed rune-marked box, a small iron lockbox, and a trunk of spare clothes, all hinting at the witch’s secrets.
  • Shadows lunged at Heyou while Jamal was ambushed by a tick the size of an armadillo.
  • Sylvie, tapping into her newfound magic, unleashed a spell that sent a foul wind over the tick. Jamal swung his warhammer, cracking the creature’s shell.
  • Heyou downed a health potion, feeling the strength surge, while Draz took down one of the shadowy figures with a precise arrow.
  • The tick drained Jamal’s vitality, but Sylvie’s magic finally put it down. Heyou used his Power Push to send the tick tumbling off a cliff.
  • After a long rest, we regrouped. Sylvie tried to heal Heyou’s wound, but her magic faltered.
  • We opened the rune-marked box, revealing a circlet, a layered necklace, and a signet ring, all with magical properties.

We debated our next steps—Askavir eggs or back to Ulmira’s Step. Sylvie shot down the idea of chasing the witch, fearing more losses and declaring our quest for the Blessed Branch a bust.

  • Draz recalled the fate of the soul-blighted, while Jamal checked a small stone figure, finding it inert.
  • We decided to follow the mountain crest southwest, avoiding Ulmira’s Step for now. An avalanche hit while we were near the snowline, but we braced together and came out unscathed. Draz’s compass acted up, only aligning when “north” pointed skyward.
  • We kept climbing, with Draz setting ropes and Jamal leaping up. Sylvie faced a close call with large eagle-like birds but regained her footing.
  • We camped under a pictograph, where Sylvie cast a tarot reading, questioning her existence and future. During Draz’s watch, he spotted a distant sacred pyre glowing on a peak, accompanied by a mournful sound echoing across the slopes.
  • With morning light, we moved toward the pyre, running into a feral simiah. Draz steeled himself and ended its suffering with a well-placed arrow.
  • By afternoon, we reached Verrow's Span, a cliffside village where the locals welcomed us. Draz was called “cousin,” and we were offered food and rest.
  • Sylvie consulted a scholar named Tamber about the magical items, but he couldn’t identify them. Heyou found references to “vessels” in Tamber’s books, hinting at deeper connections to our quest.

As night fell, we watched the moon, a void with something moving behind it, leaving us with more questions than answers. The costs of our journey weighed heavy—Sylvie’s heart still missing, and the witch Ygva’s threat looming over us like a dark cloud.
