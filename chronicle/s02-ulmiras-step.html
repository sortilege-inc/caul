Ulmira's Step

Session 02·2025-07-21

Summary

The Enduring Lesser Lights arrived at Ulmira’s Step, a settlement nestled in the fossilized bones of a titan, seeking a Blessed Branch to rekindle the dead pyre of Drosven’s Gate.

  • Sylvie shared the town’s history: it was the first stair of a pilgrimage road to a shrine at the mountain’s peak, before a collapse buried most of the steps. Founded by giants and later joined by humans and dwarves, it keeps its own dialect (Lammat) and customs. She named the raptor overhead in the local tongue — the Askavir — and urged the party to tread lightly on unfamiliar ground.
  • Crossing the bridge, the road’s miasma thinned; children of giants and dwarves played in the square, and a gong called everyone to a communal midday meal cooked by the giant chef, Kelbar.
  • Heyou spotted Syndicate operators and made contact with Borek, a dwarf flaunting a heavy conjoined ring. Borek knew where one of Heyou’s missing limbs was and wanted Askavir eggs in exchange — the great bird does not breed, but lays eggs rumored to hold the last vestiges of the departed gods. He also draped Heyou with a heavy silver ward-medallion — a snarling figure ringed in metal — not a key, but a charm to keep him from being eaten by the Askavir.
  • A battered delving party returned from the titan’s tunnels — five of eight or nine — and Sylvie’s offer of magical healing was politely but firmly refused. Their famed guide, the ribbit Tamros, did not return; he had once gone into those same ruins with the witch Ygva and found something she carried up the mountain.
  • By the fire, Draz helped a survivor named Mellon pick shards of glass shrapnel from his chest and back — clumsily driving a few deeper. Mellon described the tunnel: heavily trapped by someone who reset the traps behind them, roughly a mile and a half in over a week, people lost and nothing found. He means to return better-prepared, and welcomes company.
  • Sylvie sought out Yol, an old giantess with a faint spark of Splendor, who explained the town’s wariness: since the gods left, the community rejects Splendor-domain magic — healing among it — as an unwelcome reminder of what abandoned them. Yol confirmed the current witch is Ygva, apprentice of the older witch Oshel whom Sylvie had once heard of; that the moon-rune Jara from Sylvie’s visions is Ygva’s rune; and named Sarru the pyre-keeper as the one who could guide them. Looking up afterward, Sylvie sensed a piece of the moon slowly approaching.
  • Heyou worked a dwarf fence, Gala, for intel: the tunnel’s trap-setters are almost certainly goblins — master trap- and explosive-makers — and an underground community that once lived and traded down there fell silent some twenty years ago.
  • The party met Sarru — a fit, well-outfitted dwarf pyre-keeper — at the Sacred Pyre. She could guide them only as far as she ranges for pyre-tinder, then point the way to Ygva, and warned them to decide what price they would pay before meeting her (“I’ve never seen her take payment in money”). Ygva, she said, had been inseparable from Kelun — an old talking construct-statue from before the gods left, who learned of the Amber Reach and of magic from the Celsians — and had apprenticed under Oshel.
  • Weighing a costly ascent to Ygva against the trapped tunnels where she first found her branch, the party chose to go down first — to delve the titan’s ruins with Mellon’s crew, hoping to win leverage, or the branch itself, before facing the witch. Borek sponsored tier-two gear for the whole party, on loan.

Loose ends: the townsfolk’s wariness, the price Ygva will name, the goblins in the dark below, and the ever-present shadow of the Askavir.
