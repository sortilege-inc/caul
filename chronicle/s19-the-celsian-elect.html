The Celsian Elect

Session 19·2026-05-07

Summary

As the sun dipped below the horizon, we had a lead on the dean at the Celsian Compound. Sylvie made the call to teleport us there, but not before she dismissed Rhys with a sharp word.

  • With a swirl of black smoke, we arrived at Celsius, greeted by Dagga Brunnvar, an antiquarian dwarf who laid down the law: teleporters had to cough up artifacts for the treasury.
  • Heyou stepped up, offering his spare wooden leg, a clanker-made limb that piqued Dagga's interest. He hinted that the Keeper Of Splendor would want to see Heyou.
  • Dagga told us to leave Pinchy outside, where staff penned him in. I spotted a vault-like door that hummed with magic, but Dagga clammed up, claiming it was the dean's secret.
  • As we settled into the student barracks, Dagga pointed out the Sacred Pyre atop a sheer tower, the air thick with magic and shadows lurking in the corridors.
  • Heyou turned invisible, prowling the halls until Dean Therion Calanthe caught him off guard and magically displaced him to a dark, metal chamber where Ygva lay unconscious.
  • Draz climbed the compound's exterior, spotting Pinchy penned by carts and overhearing guardians discussing "the heir," raising questions about their significance.
  • Jamal picked up a stand-up violin, but Dagga snatched it back, then took Jamal to a higher-status dining room to bond over drinks and loyalty.
  • Sylvie mapped the magical emanations, noting concentrations around the temple, the dean's area, and the pyre's core, before being summoned by the Keeper Of Splendor.
  • In the Barn, Sylvie met Inola, a middle-aged Simia radiating Splendor, who invited her to dinner, filled with warmth and camaraderie.

As evening fell, Dagga secured us an audience with the dean. Draz joined us in the Audience Hall, where multiple copies of Dean Therion awaited. He grilled us about Ygva, and Sylvie explained our quest for a sacred branch to rekindle our township's pyre. Just then, Sylvie revived Ygva with healing magic, and Inola declared Ygva had brought forth two candidates for Ascension—Heyou and Sylvie—while making it clear Draz and Jamal were out of the running.

Sylvie challenged the idea of self-salvation at the expense of others, but Inola insisted only The Elect could ascend, leaving the rest to face entropy and death. Dean Therion cut in, asserting the chosen were fixed and while he might use our power, he wouldn’t accept us as eternal companions.

Tensions flared when Dean Therion launched an attack, striking Heyou and ricocheting the bolt to hit Sylvie.

  • Draz tried to shoot Therion, but his arrow passed through an illusory double.
  • Jamal charged in but missed, and the fight escalated with Ash-begets-Tide targeting Ygva with entropic magic.
  • Sylvie unleashed a fireball, but it fizzled out, and the battle raged on with Jamal and Dagga joining the fray.
  • Draz’s keen aim dispelled one of Therion's duplicates, revealing the true Therion's location.
  • The ground rumbled as Heyou managed to stab Therion, bringing him down.

Just when victory seemed within reach, the ground erupted beneath us, heralding the arrival of a Runt God. We were left amidst allies and uncertain neutrals, with Ygva's remains now corrupted into sapling treants.

The cost? Trust shattered, alliances frayed, and a new threat looming over us. The case was far from closed.
