The Celsian Vaults

Session 22·2026-06-09

Summary

In the fragile safety of the half-relit pyre, the Enduring Lesser Lights caught their breath, took stock of Heyou's stolen treasures, and let Sylvie pour the whole mad story into Inola's ear — enough to shake the Keeper of Splendor into rethinking her plans. Then Inola led them down into the Athenaeum's vaults to weigh what the collection held, and the deeper they went the stranger it got: a library that shoots trespassers, a map that predicts where power hides, and — frozen in the lowest room — a face Draz had never met but somehow knew.

  • The rod's fire held at a minimal, stable radius, warding the courtyard and adjacent halls while worse things loomed just beyond the light; rebuilding the full Sacred Pyre would take Dagga a crew of twenty and a week's work.
  • Draz shrugged off his bird shape as if nothing had happened, remembering nothing of the flight — a shapeshifting none of the party had ever seen from him.
  • Sylvie told Inola everything, from Drosven's Gate to Ygva's plan to her own ripped-out heart; the Keeper of Splendor found the pieces fitting her own strangest knowledge too well to dismiss, and conceded the compound was no longer secure.
  • Heyou doled out his Stacks haul — an unstable arcane shard for Draz, a fire jar and a Lore Keeper tome for Jamal, a portal seed and a potion of stability for Sylvie.
  • Bonding with the Runt God, Draz hugged its leg and came away moving in triplicate — three copies of himself, slightly out of sync and unsettling to watch; he left Pinchie behind to keep the god occupied.
  • Alone with her thoughts, Sylvie tried to reach Ygva in meditation, but the Godshard tore through her mind like a thing loose in a library, scattering her focus and slipping away when she noticed it — no dream would come.
  • Inola led them into the Stacks, a warehouse-deep repository of the world's salvaged knowledge, and downward past face-carved columns and stone Sentinels that fired on Heyou for lifting "unauthorized material," driving him to hide invisible against the very pillars.
  • Sylvie found a bench of stoneweaver focus-stones and took one that answered her touch as her own dead stone no longer did; testing it on the constructs, she realized her lost stone-magic ran on instinct, not the bookish knowledge she had drilled as a wizard.
  • Drawn to a mannequin bearing old armor, Draz was addressed by a ghostly face that named him a survivor of "the basement below," where he and his father had gone — reopening the thread of the father and twin he never knew, said to have travelled west to the Amber Reach.
  • In the map room Inola showed them a chart of the known world and explained the Athenaeum's craft: powerful artifacts and beings cluster in predictable geometries, and with the Runt God's protection the party might reach the far, guarded sites the archivists never could — she handed Heyou the log of an expedition that lost some sixty-odd of eighty.
  • The Stoneheart Peaks shelves had been stripped nearly bare — one broken-spined book naming Ulmira's Step, another with its middle torn out — while Sylvie struck a mother-lode of old-gods theology to study later.
  • In the lowest room, past a mounted skeletal dragon, Draz found two frozen simia — one an elder who, thawed by Heyou's suppression amulet, wore a token of Heyou's old Syndicate and asked why Draz wore the face of his child. As the second began to thaw, the skeletal dragon woke.

Costs: little blood spilled, but the map-room's guardian — a mounted skeletal dragon — roused mid-thaw and set upon them, the session ending in the thick of that fight. Gains: Heyou's trove and the expedition log; Jamal's Lore Keeper tome; Sylvie's answering focus-stone and a lead on old-gods lore; and, most of all, Draz's frozen father and twin, found at last. Loose ends: the two thawing simia and their tie to Heyou's Syndicate; the "basement below" of Draz's buried past; the stripped Stoneheart Peaks records; and Inola's offer to send the party — shielded by the Runt God — after the world's far, hidden powers.
