Frozen Kin, a Forbidden Library

Session 23·2026-07-16

Summary

Picking up in the thick of the map-room fight, the Enduring Lesser Lights put down the skeletal dragon and finally thawed the two figures frozen beside it — and for Draz the ice gave back a father and a twin he had never known, both agents of Heyou's old Syndicate. From there the delve went deeper still, into a forbidden library of ghost-keepers and a great watching eye, where Sylvie spat out the thing that had ridden her mind and came away with a servant of her own.

  • Jamal and Draz — the latter still shifted into a wolf and split into three flickering copies — finished the skeletal dragon; Sylvie siphoned out the seed of magic animating it, and it toppled dead, while the sparks it had shed befriended Jamal and trailed him like a chain of lights.
  • The two frozen simia thawed and were named: Vanco, the hard elder who wore a token of Heyou's Syndicate — Draz's father — and Zevin, the younger, who greeted Draz as the twin he had always half-remembered. Both had been sent to the Athenaeum to learn how Heyou, a construct, was made.
  • Vanco returned to Heyou a device drawn from his own chest — the core that had once given him motion — and, still bent on the heist that had frozen him, agreed to press deeper with Heyou's help.
  • Draz slapped his father for leaving him and was answered with cold steel and a colder promise; only later, thawing, did Vanco admit that necessity had split the family, each parent taking a twin, and that he had long since given up hope of seeing his son again.
  • Following Inola and a resonance only Sylvie could feel on the world-map, the party broke into the forbidden library — a bottomless well of shelves patrolled by ghost-librarians and a vast, watching eye that blasted Draz the moment he shouted.
  • Climbing the shelves, Sylvie at last expelled the thing she had swallowed from the dead dean: it fluttered from her mouth as shrieking imps, freeing her of its grip even as it tore through the stacks and hurled itself at the eye.
  • Amid comic disaster — Vanco falling from a ledge, Zevin throwing a rope without holding its other end — Draz ran the walls to haul his father back up, and Heyou lifted a librarian's spellbook clean out of its hands.
  • Jamal ended the eye with a single thrown arcane shard, a record blast that shattered it and rained gore across the whole company.
  • Descending past the light into the dark below, Sylvie sensed Ygva somewhere deeper, took a tome someone had nailed shut, and gave a piece of her own Umbra to a faceless stone servant that woke, named itself for her purpose, and followed her up — she called it Speaker.
  • Their heist unfinished, Vanco and Zevin turned back down with a promise to return; Inola, meanwhile, had slipped away in the chaos and could not be found.
  • Out at last, Draz spent the long rest teaching the Runt God to greet him gently, Heyou passed Sylvie the stolen ghost-book, and the company bedded down with Dagga and Pinchie to read what the vaults had given them.

Costs: nothing dead that mattered — the guardians were the Athenaeum's own — but Inola, the party's guide and the Keeper of Splendor, has vanished into the vaults. Gains: Draz's father and twin, alive and owed a reunion; Sylvie freed of the Godshard and attended now by the servant Speaker, plus a nailed tome and a librarian's book to study; Heyou made whole with his returned core; and a lead on Sylvie's teleportation — she can reach the far, powerful places if only she can first see them, in a true painting. Loose ends: where Inola has gone; what Vanco and Zevin will find of Heyou's making below; the Ygva-sense at the bottom of the well; and the paintings of the Amber Reach and the Ashen Seas the party still needs to travel abroad.
