Session

Ka-blooey

Session 05·2025-09-11

Summary

In the depths of the caverns, we faced the remnants of our recent battles—goblins and a Guardian beast that had given us more than a few headaches. The goblins had set a trap, rigging a bridge with Explosive Barrels. They warned that those who tried to use explosives against the beast never returned.

  • Heyou haggled with the goblins to repurpose the explosives, despite the creature's long reach.
  • The Guardian beast showed its cunning, repositioning itself and complicating our plans.
  • During camp, Mellon’s ghost showed up with his cat, Yuki. Sylvie tried to pry answers from him, but he just smiled sadly.
  • Savel managed to get through to Mellon, who wanted to stay with Yuki but was ready to help us against the beast.
  • We revised our strategy: use Mellon as a distraction while moving the explosives closer.
  • Heyou moved the barrels, but the beast spotted him and charged, cornering him on the bridge.
  • Draz fired at an explosive barrel, triggering a massive blast that injured Heyou and severed the beast's mystical link to the tree.
  • The Guardian beast lunged at Jamal, who struck back hard with his warhammer.
  • Sylvie pushed the beast into another barrel’s path, causing a second explosion that took it down for good.

We thought we’d won, but Jamal delivered more strikes to ensure it was finished, and Heyou decapitated it, revealing a viscous fluid instead of blood. Among the remains, we found a Giant Collar, a relic of its connection to the now-dead Sacred Tree. We retrieved the Blessed Branch it had carried and additional branches, while Heyou noticed seedlike objects around the tree’s base—fire-triggered seed containers, Sylvie said.

Victory didn’t last long. The goblins returned, armed and eager to set more explosives. Sylvie tried to protect the Sacred Tree, but the Goblin Leader laughed her off. Heyou suggested using the tree's destruction as leverage. Then, a goblin fumbled with an explosive barrel, and the Sacred Tree was obliterated in a catastrophic blast, tossing us aside. Mellon and Yuki's spirits appeared, watching the destruction unfold.

With urgency in the air, we decided to bail before more goblins showed up, taking the three blessed branches as our only memento of the tree’s power. The Goblin Leader was presumed lost in the chaos. As we tried to escape, a stone figure named Kelun blocked our path, different from the previous stone wraiths. He spoke into Sylvie’s mind, condemning the destruction and demanding we follow him to face the music.

Sylvie proposed splitting up: some would take the branches to Drosven’s Gate while she and Heyou followed Kelun. Jamal and Draz would escort Savel and Sarru to Drosven’s Gate, while Sylvie and Heyou would scale the mountains with Kelun. During the climb, Sylvie revealed her reluctance about returning to the mountains, haunted by painful memories, hoping Ygva could shed light on her connection to the magic that once bound her to stone.

Meanwhile, Jamal and Draz had their own troubles when an Askavir, a giant supernatural raptor, snatched them up. Savel cast a protective spell on Jamal while Draz wrestled with fear and awe during the flight. The Askavir revealed its divine purpose of delivering them before dropping them on separate mountain peaks, setting off individual survival challenges.

Kelun explained the nature of divine beings and the pantheon of eight gods, revealing he was a mouthpiece for Caranthalis and others, shedding light on the gods' absence and the intricacies of divine communication.

Costs: a Sacred Tree gone, a Goblin Leader presumed dead, and lingering questions about Mellon’s ghost and our fate. Loose ends: what awaits us at the mountains, and what will happen when we face the consequences of our actions?
