A Blazing Sun

Session 20·2026-05-21

Summary

The floor of the Celsian Athenaeum's Audience Hall still gaped where the Runt God had torn through it, and the Enduring Lesser Lights found themselves ringed by uneasy almost-allies: Ygva dead with saplings sprouting from her corpse, Casper tol-Otheidas summoning angels to put the erupted god down, and the Keeper of Splendor recoiling from a thing she had never seen. What followed was a frantic four-way scramble over whether the Runt God should be killed, caged, or comforted — and it ended with Draz, of all of them, crowned in a dead heir's regalia and able to see Splendor itself.

  • Casper tol-Otheidas, resolve hardening across his face, summoned a pair of Junior Seraphs — biblically grim angels of maces and floating orb-clutching hands — and bent them toward killing the Runt God, shouting for Dagga to fetch the scepter from the Sacred Pyre.
  • Heyou traded on his old Syndicate ties to reach Ash-begets-Tide, arguing the Runt God was too pivotal to kill; the fungal priest answered by lodging clamps of stone around the creature, pinning it in place and leaving it vulnerable.
  • The Runt God's Reality Quake left Sylvie and Draz "unstuck from reality." A pseudopod folded gently over Sylvie's mouth — not to smother her, but the way a lonely thing reaches for kin; through her Siphon Essence she felt it seeking belonging, harmless in intent yet liable to kill her by accident.
  • Heyou slipped behind Casper tol-Otheidas into a chokehold while Draz and Jamal landed a pair of critical blows that all but erased him. Casper's dying breath was a magical command, calling still more Junior Seraphs to guard his corpse.
  • Draz pried a small, plainly magical circlet from Casper's brow and set it on his own head — and a new sense opened in him: he perceived Splendor as light and aura, the Runt God blazing brightest of all, Sylvie glowing a lesser echo of the same.
  • Inola, the Keeper of Splendor, argued for keeping the Runt God alive as a source of Splendor, then poured out a hoarded reserve of power into a level-ten Resurrection over Ygva's remains — which guttered and failed. Ygva stayed dead; Casper tol-Otheidas, too, was now beyond return.
  • Heyou reached into Casper's opened throat to draw out the Splendor there, as he once had from the Askavir's blood — but the corpse won the tug-of-war, leaving him depleted of Splendor magic and a slick of gold crawling up his arm.
  • Dagga came crashing back through the glass wall astride Pinchie, bearing the scepter from the pyre; the seraphs heaved Casper's body onto the Runt God to be absorbed, but Sylvie force-pushed it clear and Heyou turned the corpse invisible while the party, the crab, and Inola cut the last angels down.
  • Jamal, battered by the thing's flailing, "booped the snoot" — using Hideous Retribution to hurt the Runt God precisely enough to seize its attention, then speaking to it.
  • Inola cupped Draz's face — "My son. It is a blessing. Take it." — and Dagga pressed the scepter into his hands. As Draz grasped it his new sight bloomed from a single note into a full symphony: power alive in himself, in Inola, its vestiges in Heyou, a roaring fire in Sylvie, and the Runt God a blazing sun he could not look upon.
  • Draz cast Nature's Tongue and projected calm; the Runt God, terrified of him yet yearning to be near, folded in on itself and went quiet. Between Draz and Sylvie the party now held a fragile thread of communication with a god.

Costs: Casper tol-Otheidas dead by Heyou's blade and unrecoverable; Heyou depleted of Splendor magic until a long rest and marked by the gold that crawled from the corpse; Inola's Resurrection spent for nothing. In hand: the circlet that lets Draz see Splendor, the scepter that powers the Celsian Athenaeum's Sacred Pyre, and some of Casper's Splendor-rich blood on Heyou. Loose ends: what to do with the subdued Runt God — kill it to fuel an Ascension as Casper intended, cage it to widen the Athenaeum's protection as Inola and Ash-begets-Tide urge, or carry it and the gathered power down into the World Below to enact Ygva's plan, which the party is warned would demand a sacrifice. Dean Therion Calanthe lies unconscious but alive, kept as leverage; four very different roads open ahead.

Timeline
