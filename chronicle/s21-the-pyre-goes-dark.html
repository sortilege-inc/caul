The Pyre Goes Dark

Session 21·2026-06-02

Summary

With Casper tol-Otheidas dead and the Runt God uneasily calmed, the Enduring Lesser Lights took stock inside the Celsian Athenaeum — only to learn that the scepter Draz now carried was the very thing that fed the settlement's Sacred Pyre, and that pulling it had left the whole compound naked to the Umbra. As monsters poured in and the dead refused to stay down, the party split: some racing to rekindle the pyre, others defending the vaults. By the end Draz wore feathers, Sylvie carried a demon, and Casper tol-Otheidas was gone for good.

  • With the fighting lulled, Heyou stripped the unconscious Dean Therion Calanthe of a dozen magical rings, a stave, and a heavy quartz-and-bead pendant that proved not merely inert but magic-suppressing — a bidirectional dampener Heyou slowly learned to slip his own casting through.
  • Inola recognized the circlet on Draz's brow as the crown of Otheidas's heir and marveled that neither it nor the Beast had rejected him — "it is in your bloodline too" — a claim Draz, lost in the new lights only he could see, could not answer.
  • Word spread that the scepter Dagga had fetched was what powered the Sacred Pyre; with it gone, the bulwark against the old Umbra had failed, and screams rose from the dark outside. Casper tol-Otheidas rose again as an Umbral undead, and Dean Therion Calanthe began to stir, something burning behind his eyes.
  • The party split three ways: Draz, Jamal, and Inola made for the pyre tower; Dagga bolted to guard the vaulted Stacks; Heyou and Sylvie cut down the risen Casper tol-Otheidas — hurled inside-out into the Runt God, which drank him up through a proboscis and ended him for good.
  • Sylvie siphoned the essence of the possessed Dean Therion Calanthe and, choosing to let the pull run its course, drained him dry — killing him but drawing the raging thing inside him into herself. She now houses a Godshard: an angry, confused entity that leaves her fear sharper and more dangerous.
  • Racing to defend the Stacks, Sylvie tore the Umbral corruption out of a mob of transformed students like black smoke, uncorrupting rather than killing them — a power none of them had seen. Dagga revealed the Stacks to be the Athenaeum's great repository of lost knowledge and artifacts, a hedge against a failing world.
  • Heyou, his suppression amulet swallowing the vault's deadly wards, slipped into the Stacks — an eight- or nine-level warren of statues, tomes, and half-restored relics, nearly everything humming with Splendor — and filled a bottomless bag with consumables, arcane shards, a replication parchment, and more.
  • At the tower, Draz drove single-mindedly across a bridge menaced by flaming skulls and a lightning-wreathed albatross and jammed the scepter into its slot — taking the bird's lightning through his body but sparking the Sacred Pyre back to life.
  • The Runt God burst from the building and made for the tower; as Draz flung himself off the top to reach it, the creature struck him with a mote of its power while Sylvie's force-push redirected his fall — and Draz took the shape of a great bird, catching flight for the first time.
  • The Runt God tore the albatross apart and cut the legs from the tower; Jamal tackled Inola onto a rooftop as the bridge collapsed, and Draz, now winged, fanned the guttering pyre where it had fallen into the rubble.

Costs: Dean Therion Calanthe dead, the Godshard drawn from him now lodged in Sylvie; Casper tol-Otheidas absorbed by the Runt God and beyond any return; the pyre tower toppled and its fire sputtering in the plaza rubble rather than blazing overhead. Gains: Heyou's bag of pilfered Stacks treasures and Therion's suppression amulet; Draz's new bird form and hinted-at Otheidas blood; Sylvie's power to cleanse Umbral corruption. Loose ends: the Godshard Sylvie now carries and the powers it may bring; whether Draz is truly of the god-king's line; and the exposed Athenaeum, its half-relit pyre, and a Runt God loose in its ruins.
