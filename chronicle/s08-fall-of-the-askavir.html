Fall of the Askavir

Session 08·2025-10-17

Summary

The party rolled into Verrow's Span, a settlement buzzing with life. We were here for answers, but trouble was lurking in every shadow.

  • Heyou asked the locals about Dimmers, heard whispers about the Dimmers, and kept his guard up.
  • Jamal jumped into a drinking contest with a local Simiah and ended up face down on the floor, drunk and humiliated.
  • Sylvie buried herself in the runes of Tamber, linking the Rune of the Moon to false hope and paralysis, connecting it to Tlellic, the god of predation, and sparking her interest in the Askavir, a giant bird tied to that god.
  • Draz caught the eye of a Village Elder who recognized his birthmark, revealing a dark family history—his mother dead, abandoned in hard times.
  • A flood of thirty refugees from Dolm's Break crashed into town, doubling the population and bringing grim news: their sacred pyre was extinguished, and Umbral Beasts had overrun their home.
  • A tall feline katari appeared, a ghost from Heyou’s past with Dimmers, just as the Askavir cast its shadow over the settlement.
  • Panic erupted as the Askavir swooped down, threatening the refugees. Sylvie acted fast, casting Tether to ground the beast momentarily.
  • The Askavir retaliated, and Draz landed a shot right in its eye. Jamal dashed in to rescue a captured refugee while chaos reigned—civilians fell as the beast rampaged.
  • Sylvie unleashed a Fireball just as a wyrm ascended, igniting both creatures in a blinding explosion.
  • Draz took another critical shot, sending the Askavir crashing down, while Sylvie siphoned life from the wyrm, and Heyou executed a Molotov drop, finishing the wyrm in a fiery spectacle.

When the dust settled, we stood over the Askavir's carcass, feeling the magic pulsing from its heart. Ygva, a witch, and the Grim Pyromancer showed up, laying claim to the prize. Heyou tried to ambush Ygva, but things turned ugly fast. Draz opened fire, and Ygva retaliated, severing Heyou's arms and unleashing a wave of destruction.

Sylvie fumbled to cast another spell, the heart’s aura throwing her off. Ygva grabbed the heart, taunting us as she made her escape. Heyou swiped it during her distraction, and we regrouped, contemplating our next move. Draz wanted to chase Ygva, convinced she was wounded and vulnerable, but Heyou urged caution, reminding us of the bigger crisis at hand.

We debated strategy, with Heyou suggesting we craft a fake heart using ichor from the Askavir's eye. Tamber chimed in with insights about Ygva’s past—she had trained with him before being expelled for her dangerous practices.

Damages: a severed arm for Heyou, the weight of lost lives, and the heart slipping through our fingers. Loose ends: Ygva on the run, the refugee crisis unresolved, and the Askavir’s legacy still haunting us.
