Borek's Stash

Session 12·2025-12-19

Summary

In the shadowy alleys of Ulmira’s Step, I had a job to do. Heyou, a clank rogue with a knack for stealth, brought Draz and Sylvie into the fold to infiltrate Borek’s residence. We needed information and missing parts from the Syndicate fixer. Meanwhile, Jamal was busy trying to charm a massive horned beast named Kalisk into loyalty.

  • Draz kicked off the distraction, spinning an elaborate monologue to keep Borek occupied.
  • Borek’s eyes narrowed when he caught wind of Draz’s ties to a group that dabbled in explosives.
  • Heyou cast invisibility on Sylvie, and they slipped through an unbolted window into a cramped bedroom stuffed with locked chests.
  • An Umbra crow appeared, responding to Sylvie’s thoughts, as they scouted the room.
  • They spotted a guarded door and a dark, unguarded one, sensing a magical ward on the latter.
  • Heyou picked the lock and suppressed the ward, gaining access to a room filled with crates and weapons.
  • Sylvie detected a remnant of Splendor magic, hinting that the good stuff had been cleared out recently.
  • Jamal managed to collar Kalisk, leading it back to camp, though it preferred thorny weeds over the offered grasses.
  • Borek confronted Draz about the missing families from Drosven’s Gate, revealing a binding rune on Draz’s arm that could cloud memories.
  • Borek offered a translucent purple gem, trying to recruit Draz into the Syndicate to counteract malign influences, but Draz turned him down.

As night deepened, Jamal spotted mutated townsfolk, including a katari brewer in beast form, moving toward Ulmira’s Step. He stayed alert, ready to mount Kalisk if things went south. In the bunk room, Heyou found one of his missing prosthetic legs, marked with a symbol tied to Brathis, a deity of feral survival. He handed Sylvie a fear-inducing Devouring Dagger and secured an Axe of Fortunus for Jamal.

Just as we were about to make our exit, a shadowy figure blocked our path, demanding payment. Sylvie used Power Push to hurl the assailant through the wall, but chaos erupted as guards swarmed in. Sylvie got nicked by her own dagger, and Heyou detonated an area strike, taking out the simiah pinning her and wounding others. Borek took off, and the remaining guards decided discretion was the better part of valor.

Jamal roused Kalisk, and they headed for the road while Draz got ambushed by the mutated townsfolk. He bolted for the trees, taking some hits along the way. As Sylvie and Heyou exited Borek’s building with the stolen goods, they ran into a monstrous crowd of familiar faces, now twisted by mutation. The Umbra crow trailed them as they melted into the shadows.

The night left us with injuries, a dented trust, and the looming threat of the Syndicate’s wrath. With Sarru and Savel among the mutated townsfolk, we faced a tangled web of loyalties and dangers ahead. We’d snagged some valuable artifacts, but the road ahead was riddled with uncertainty and the specter of betrayal.
