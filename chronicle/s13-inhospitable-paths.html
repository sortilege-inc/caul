Inhospitable Paths

Session 13·2026-01-02

Summary

We were in the thick of it, ready to ambush the shamblers from Drosven’s Gate. Draz, the shadowy archer, melted into the trees. Jamal, the dwarf warrior, kept a low profile on his You Bastard, tailing a group on the road. Sylvie, the fire-wielding mage, and Heyou, the rogue-warlock tinkerer, took their positions, eyes locked on the approaching undead.

  • Sylvie recognized the shamblers as Umbra-distorted beings, including our former allies Sarru and Savel.
  • Heyou pushed for a knockout plan, but Sylvie wanted to gather more intel.
  • Savel began casting a spell, binding Sylvie and Heyou in unseen restraints.
  • Sylvie broke free under the moonlight; Heyou used his ancient-tech savvy to escape.
  • Sylvie unleashed a Fireball, igniting four foes and throwing them into disarray.
  • Jamal charged in, but Savel’s magic held him back.
  • Sylvie retaliated against Sarru with Hideous Retribution as the kalisk bucked under pressure.
  • Heyou cast Blades Everywhere, taking down Savel and Sarru non-lethally.
  • Draz provided cover fire from the trees until Sylvie’s Fireball set his perch ablaze.

Jamal broke free and executed a double front-flip dismount, crushing the remaining horde with his Improved Warhammer. Sylvie finished off a survivor with Siphon Essence, while Jamal dealt the final blow to the last enemy. We regrouped, and Sylvie insisted we leave. Draz invoked Healing Fields to patch us up.

Draz calmed the kalisk, discovering it preferred spiny thistles. Heyou secured Savel and Sarru for transport. We debated our next move, weighing the risks of returning to Ulmira’s Step against the dangers of Grithmaar Deep. We decided to return to our old campsite for the Living Fortress and its Blessed Branch.

  • We reached the campsite but were ambushed by a night Dragon.
  • The Dragon breathed fire, scorching everyone but Heyou.
  • Sylvie retaliated with Hideous Retribution as the Dragon grappled the kalisk.
  • Jamal leapt onto the kalisk, injuring the Dragon’s wing and forcing it to land.
  • Draz executed a Trick Shot, blinding one of the Dragon’s eyes.
  • The Dragon lashed out at Sylvie and Heyou, but they dodged its attacks.
  • Sylvie used Siphon Essence to restore herself while Jamal and Draz blinded the Dragon’s other eye.
  • Heyou attempted a backstab but failed to pierce the Dragon’s hide.
  • Sylvie detonated a close-range Fireball, killing the Dragon but sending the kalisk into a panic.

After the fight, Draz and Heyou salvaged parts from the Dragon’s corpse, collecting scales and volatile organs. We organized our travel and set our course toward Drosven’s Gate, but the streets were crawling with non-humanoid monsters and no townsfolk in sight.

  • We debated relighting a Sacred Pyre but lacked the know-how without a priest.
  • Heyou suggested bypassing Drosven’s Gate, and we skirted the settlement, heading for the mountain approach.
  • Jamal recalled the vast trade concourse where outsiders once mingled with dwarves.
  • We decided to camp halfway into the tunnel for security, with the Living Fortress providing our only greenery.

As we settled in for a long rest, Draz integrated Dragon scales into his armor. We remained encamped within the mountain trade tunnel, ready to delve deeper into Grithmaar Deep and tackle whatever awaited us.

Costs: injuries from the Dragon, a panicked You Bastard, and the lingering threat of the monsters in Drosven’s Gate. Loose ends: Savel and Sarru’s fate, the uncertainty of the Sacred Pyre, and the dangers lurking in Grithmaar Deep.
