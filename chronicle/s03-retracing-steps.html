Retracing Steps

Session 03·2025-08-13

Summary

The Enduring Lesser Lights set out from Ulmira’s Step to delve the newly-opened passage beneath the titan — where the town’s own delvers had been trapped, and where the witch Ygva once found her branch — hoping to win leverage, or a Blessed Branch itself, before facing her.

  • Still reluctant, Sylvie came along, sharing only that the witch drew on the Umbra and would want a price. She cast wards — investing Hope into shimmering armor for the party — and gave Heyou the scarf she had knitted, promising socks next. Three of Ulmira’s Step’s people came with them: Mellon, a human ranger, and his snow leopard Yuki; Savel, a giant sorcerer who had delved with Mellon before; and Sarru the pyre-keeper, taking up a guardian’s role. Mellon’s directions kept shifting — the way proved less an enclosed tunnel than a narrow crevasse between two peaks, opened when a rockfall shifted.
  • Descending the switchback steps, Sylvie recognized reliefs of the departed gods Tlellic (the long night, predation) and Kreth (false visions, deceit, the moon). Draz found a dark, kerosene-reeking slick on the walls; Heyou knew it from the forge — the flammable secretion of an ooze — and warned the passage was laced with fuel.
  • A goblin archer’s arrow whistled past Jamal, and an ambush sprang: a near-ten-foot goblinoid brute, warped by the Umbra, charged with a club and hammered Jamal. Heyou struck from its blind side; the ichor ignited the wall-slicks and fire raced up the stairs. A flammable ooze-creature burst overhead — Draz’s critical shot let him shove Sylvie clear of the blast.
  • A goblin stabbed Sylvie from behind; Draz cut it down. The party overwhelmed the brute — Savel’s arcane gauntlets landing the finishing blow — though it collapsed hurling a boulder that battered Draz, Sarru, and Sylvie. Later it dragged itself up again, glowing with Umbra light, before Draz re-killed it and Jamal and Heyou destroyed its head for good.
  • On the brute’s chest were three symbols — not carved like the others but branded, meant to compel or empower — and they had glowed just before it reanimated. Sylvie recognized the power as the same Umbra-sort that opposes the sacred pyres and stands at enmity with her. Heyou cut the branded skin away to study its runes.
  • Uneasy, Sylvie drew a card — the High Priestess reversed, truths out of reach and action forced on incomplete knowledge — and warned the group that going on would bring disaster.
  • Then an explosion and rockfall ahead sealed the way forward, and Mellon — distraught — ran at the rubble and passed insubstantially through the solid stone, vanishing to the far side. Yuki could not follow, and mourned at the fresh stonefall. (Draz recalled that when he had picked the glass shrapnel from Mellon in town, some shards had been drawn into his body — dismissed then as fumbling.)
  • Draz and Heyou scaled a goblin lookout-ledge under arrow fire: red sconces at intervals, half a dozen goblins hammering, stringing lines, and fortifying a kill-corridor around a pale glow perhaps a mile deeper in. Rather than push straight through, the party resolved to bait and ambush the goblins — feigning a departure up the stairs, Draz hidden, to strike when they came to collect the brute’s corpse.

The cost: Mellon vanished into stone, Jamal and Draz battered, and Sylvie’s dread deepening. Loose ends: the witch’s price, the goblins’ purpose in the dark, and whatever the branded runes truly command.
