Vignette — The Whispering Deck

A tarot reading, written between sessions.

A little vignette for the Tarot, pre- Thursday:

Reaching into her worn leather pouch, Sylvie drew out her Whispering Deck. The cards hummed faintly in her palm. Perhaps, tonight, at last, they would be a mirror to the truths she sought. She shuffled, the dry rustle of the cards a familiar comfort, and laid out three for herself: Past, Present, Future.

The first card, turned for her Past, was the Tower. Sylvie’s lips thinned, a faint line of bitter frustration etched around them. Again. Always the Tower.

Its image was seared into her memory: a jagged tower struck by lightning, figures tumbling from its heights amidst chaos and ruin. To Sylvie, this card was the indelible mark of the Sundering. It wasn't just a symbol of destruction; it was the echo of her own hand in that destruction. It was the cataclysmic confrontation, the raw power that was unleashed, the moment she lost her connection to the Earth-Heart, the severance that still felt like a missing limb. The Tower was the smoking crater where her home once stood, the shattered fragments of her community, and the obliteration of her former self. It confirmed the profound, inescapable guilt that sat like a stone in her gut – a testament to her failure, her fear that her own power- what broken pieces remained of it- was inherently destructive, a burden that leveled all it touched. It was a constant finger in the wound of what she had lost, and what she had caused. As if she needed reminding.

Next, for her Present, she turned The Hermit. A sigh, barely audible, escaped her. Also predictable.

The Hermit depicted a cloaked figure, alone on a desolate peak, holding a lantern aloft. Her life since the Sundering. It spoke of solitude, introspection, and a deliberate withdrawal from the world. It was her chosen path of wandering, her avoidance of connection, and her relentless pursuit of forgotten lore. The Hermit's lantern represented her internal search for answers, her constant vigilance, and her self-imposed isolation. The card also spoke, albeit more quietly, to the profound grief she carried, the weight of a shattered past that dogged her steps, no matter how far she went. She was the seeker, but also the outcast, forever walking a solitary path. Her safest place was alone. Again - as if she needed reminding.

Finally, for her Future, the card was The Moon. Sylvie stared at it, her frustration deepening. It had been this way since the Sundering – a ceaseless cycle of these three cards, over and over.

The Moon card showed a winding path between two towers, leading towards the horizon where a full moon hung, shedding an eerie, uncertain light. Certainly, the Moon spoke directly to her new magic, but also, and more disturbingly, to mystery, intuition, and the unknown. Its light was deceptive, not illuminating truths directly, but revealing them through shadow and symbol. For her future, it meant a continued journey through uncertainty, endlessly navigating hidden dangers and illusions. Would her future forever be shrouded in uncertainty and isolation, never truly finding solid ground or clear light?

"Still the same," she murmured to the sleeping forest, the words barely a whisper. Years. Years of these same three cards, confirming her past catastrophe, her solitary present, and her mysterious, unsettling future. It was a truth, yes, but a frustratingly unchanging one - and one that only promised more danger, more ambiguity. Chaos, solitude, and endless mystery. Her future was all moonlit illusion. What she wouldn’t give to have a future defined by something other than solitude and uncertainty - and then she shut down that line of thought firmly. That, she decided, was enough self-pity for one evening, and put away the cards.
