_Dangling threads from the chronicle, still unresolved. Maintained through the stylistic rewrite — add threads as they open, and note (or strike) them as they close._

  • The cold pyre of Drosven's Gate. Still dark. It needs a Blessed Branch before the town becomes another Larrow. Three branches were won beneath the mountain — but the Askavir carried Jamal and his two up to the peak instead of down to the town, and Ygva seized all three there. The pyre remains unlit. _(S1, S5–S6)_
  • Savel and Sarru, parted from the company. The escort to Drosven's Gate broke when the Askavir snatched Jamal and Draz off the road and up to the peak; the giant sorcerer and the pyre-keeper were left behind on the way down, their fate untold. _(S5–S6)_
  • Heyou's scattered self. His true parts are somewhere in the world; he means to find them and learn why he is unlike his kind. Borek knows where one lost limb has gone — the price is Askavir eggs. One leg came back to him at the peak, summoned out of the world by Ygva to strike him; reattached, it keeps a mind of its own. _(S1–S2, S6)_
  • Ygva's price. Never coin. At the peak it came clear: she takes magic, and hearts. _(S2, S6)_
  • Sylvie's heart, taken. Ygva tore it from her chest at the peak, and she lived without it — Splendor gone out of her, Dread come into the empty place. She is the witch now, and heals by drawing the warmth out of other things. _(S6)_
  • Ygva's design. The world is a spent egg, she says; the departed gods are monsters that hatched and left the rot behind. She means to force them back down and bind them here — and Sylvie's magic is a lure that draws them. She named Sylvie "of the moon." _(S6)_
  • The traps in the titan's tunnels. Set and reset by a careful hand — almost certainly goblins. A people once lived and traded down there; they fell silent some twenty years ago. _(S2)_
  • Tamros, lost below. The ribbit guide went into the ruins with Ygva and found the thing she carried up the mountain; he did not return. _(S2)_
  • Mellon's glass. Shrapnel driven the deeper into him by mistake at the fire. _(S2)_
  • The approaching moon. Sylvie feels a piece of it drawing slowly nearer. Meaning unknown. _(S2)_
  • Sylvie's lost power. A sorcerer once, now a wizard relearning her craft. On the road the Moon came reversed, pulling her back toward the Stoneheart Peaks she fled. On the giant stair her old earth-power stirred — stone rippling under her feet — and she hoped Ygva could name what her magic was becoming, she who had once been bound to the stone and the lines of quartz. _(S1, S5)_
  • The branded runes. Three brands cut into the Umbra-warped brute, made to compel or empower; they lit before it rose from death. Heyou carries the flayed skin. What they command is unknown. _(S3)_
  • Mellon, passed into stone. He ran at a sealed rockfall and went through it, insubstantial, and walks now as a ghost beside Yuki — clearer against the world since Caranthalis's tree burned. The glass Draz drew from him in town had been drawn back into his body. _(S3–S5)_
  • Kelun's reckoning. For the destruction of Caranthalis's tree, the construct-mouthpiece compels Sylvie and Heyou up the mountain to Ygva at last. _(S5)_
  • Savel and the witch. Jamal marked hints that the giant sorcerer has ties of his own to Ygva. _(S5)_
  • The Askavir's errand. The titan-raptor did not hunt Jamal and Draz — it was _sent_ to find and deliver them, and set them down on separate peaks. By whom, and to what end, it did not say. _(S5)_
  • The door shut from this side. Kelun named the departed gods and their domains, and said the way to them seems closed; he does not know whether they can return. _(S5)_
  • The witch's sealed jewelry. Circlet, glassy-stone necklace and signet ring from Ygva's moon-locked box — unnamed by anyone yet. Tamber suggested the Celsian Athenaeum might know their make. _(S7)_
  • "Vessels." In Tamber's books Heyou found the word, beside shrines drawn to Brathis and Tlellic he had seen before, and a mandala that locked his limbs until she shut the book. What a vessel is for — and whether Sylvie's hollow chest is one — is unsaid. _(S7)_
  • North points up. Draz's compass keeps to no earthly north, only to a pole straight up past the sky. The moon hangs like a black window with something moving behind it, and Tamber's map shows the known world a mere corner of the whole. _(S7)_
  • The unopened lockbox. A keyed iron box from the witch's camp, still shut; Heyou carries it. _(S7)_
  • The coded road. The mile-high Mountain Pictographs repeat the order of the twenty-three carved stones from the witch's camp; a moon-sign is cut into the way with no door beneath it. _(S7)_
  • The Askavir's heart. A Splendor-saturated heart, cut from the fallen bird; Heyou holds it, Sylvie fears to use it, Ygva will return for it. She says one dead Askavir cannot summon the gods — but its bones might, and Draz's kin at Verrow's Span keep them against the party's return. A forged heart (ichor from the bird's eye) is planned as a decoy. _(S8)_
  • Draz's blood. An elder of Verrow's Span is his great-aunt: his mother is dead, his father and twin siblings went west to the Amber Reach, and he was given up to the Wilds on purpose while the twins were kept. _(S8)_
  • Ygva the apostate. She and Tamber were initiates together at the Celsian Athenaeum; Ygva was expelled for "depletion" — draining a thing's whole essence — which the Celsians forbid. Tamber holds full-initiation techniques Ygva never learned. Ygva calls her "sister"; Tamber calls her "apostate." _(S8)_
  • The spreading dark. Dolm's Break's pyre went out and Umbral Beasts overran it; thirty refugees fled to Verrow's Span. The failing of the pyres is widening. _(S8)_
  • The eye-patch katari. A freelance operator once tied to the Dimmers, arrived among the Dolm's Break refugees; Heyou does not trust the coincidence. _(S8)_
  • The world below. Through a hole of pale light in the cavern floor lies a dead giant-scale island city, ringed by water, lit by a false sky — the "moon behind the moon," a world beneath their own. Ygva's trail runs down through it. The party must choose: call her up, or descend after her. _(S9)_
  • Draz's amulet. A relic made at God-King Otheidas's asking to shield lesser mages from the gods; it turns a god's power (even the Askavir's) but nothing of the Umbra Ygva works. _(S9)_
  • Heyou down, the heart spent. He traded into the Askavir Heart for one god-bright smite and fell unconscious; the heart is now cold and spent, and the Living Fortress went inert with him. _(S9)_
  • The dragged titan. The river-ravine below Salthewn was gouged by a titan sliding down the mountain headfirst; its open-mouth basin catches the falls. Titans move, or moved, through this country. _(S9)_
