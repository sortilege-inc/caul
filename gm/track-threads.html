_Dangling threads from the chronicle, still unresolved. Maintained through the stylistic rewrite — add threads as they open, and note (or strike) them as they close._

  • The cold pyre of Drosven's Gate. Still dark. It needs a Blessed Branch before the town becomes another Larrow. Three branches were won beneath the mountain; two now travel down to the town with Jamal, Draz, Savel and Sarru. _(S1, S5)_
  • Heyou's scattered self. His true parts are somewhere in the world; he means to find them and learn why he is unlike his kind. Borek knows where one lost limb has gone — the price is Askavir eggs. _(S1–S2)_
  • Ygva's price. Unnamed. She has never been seen to take payment in money. _(S2)_
  • The traps in the titan's tunnels. Set and reset by a careful hand — almost certainly goblins. A people once lived and traded down there; they fell silent some twenty years ago. _(S2)_
  • Tamros, lost below. The ribbit guide went into the ruins with Ygva and found the thing she carried up the mountain; he did not return. _(S2)_
  • Mellon's glass. Shrapnel driven the deeper into him by mistake at the fire. _(S2)_
  • The approaching moon. Sylvie feels a piece of it drawing slowly nearer. Meaning unknown. _(S2)_
  • Sylvie's lost power. A sorcerer once, now a wizard relearning her craft. On the road the Moon came reversed, pulling her back toward the Stoneheart Peaks she fled. On the giant stair her old earth-power stirred — stone rippling under her feet — and she hoped Ygva could name what her magic was becoming, she who had once been bound to the stone and the lines of quartz. _(S1, S5)_
  • The branded runes. Three brands cut into the Umbra-warped brute, made to compel or empower; they lit before it rose from death. Heyou carries the flayed skin. What they command is unknown. _(S3)_
  • Mellon, passed into stone. He ran at a sealed rockfall and went through it, insubstantial, and walks now as a ghost beside Yuki — clearer against the world since Caranthalis's tree burned. The glass Draz drew from him in town had been drawn back into his body. _(S3–S5)_
  • Kelun's reckoning. For the destruction of Caranthalis's tree, the construct-mouthpiece compels Sylvie and Heyou up the mountain to Ygva at last. _(S5)_
  • Savel and the witch. Jamal marked hints that the giant sorcerer has ties of his own to Ygva. _(S5)_
  • The Askavir's errand. The titan-raptor did not hunt Jamal and Draz — it was _sent_ to find and deliver them, and set them down on separate peaks. By whom, and to what end, it did not say. _(S5)_
  • The door shut from this side. Kelun named the departed gods and their domains, and said the way to them seems closed; he does not know whether they can return. _(S5)_
