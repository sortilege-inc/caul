Location

Moon Rune Carving

The Moon Rune Carving is situated in a mountainous region, roughly five miles from a notable path adorned with large pictographs standing 20 to 30 feet tall. This carving is etched into solid rock, without any evident cave or entrance. The rugged terrain of the area shows no signs of active maintenance, suggesting the carving has existed for many years. It is associated with Ygva, a witch, indicating its potential historical or cultural significance. The climate around the carving is cold, approaching the snowline, reflective of the harsh mountainous environment.

In recent explorations, the area surrounding the carving has been linked to a vast cavern known as Moon Rune Carving, marked by the remnants of ancient titanic activity. This Moon Rune Carving features a significant hole leading into the World Below, revealing a barren expanse inhabited by massive titans engaged in the construction of an immense pyre from blessed branches and offerings. Surrounding Moon Rune Carving are remnants of a once-thriving settlement now partially demolished, adding to the area's historical intrigue. The atmosphere is imbued with residual magic, evidenced by phased creatures that turn to stone upon death.

Exploration of Moon Rune Carving includes examining the dead flying mantis-like creatures at its edge, testing their phasing properties, and strategizing for descent into the World Below. Sylvie, a witch of the umbra, surveys the area and communicates with a titan, which leads to the arrival of Valghast, a winged lion. This lion indicates a connection to a mistress commanding titans and instigates discussions about the unfolding events. The party interacts with Ygva, a powerful witch aiming to bind the gods to the inner world with the titans' aid, underscoring Moon Rune Carving's significance as a site of divine power and potential resurrection.
