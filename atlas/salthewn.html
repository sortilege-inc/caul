Location

Salthewn

Salthewn is a coastal port town known for its rugged terrain and syndicate activities. It was the base of the syndicate Heyou once ran with, and its underworld ties run deep. (The giant brothers Ugg and Grug, whom Heyou knew from those days, were muscle for a rival crew, not natives of the town.) The town is defined by its tall spires and distinctive architecture, designed to endure the coastal storms. Its grand structures rise above the landscape, showcasing the ingenuity and resilience of its inhabitants, who have adapted their building techniques to the harsh weather.

The surrounding natural features include the nearby Idol Hollows, infamous for erupting with dangerous creatures. Heyou's past in Salthewn is referenced during the chaotic eruption at the Sacred Pyre in Drosven’s Gate, linking the town to broader dangers. Notably, the caverns beneath Salthewn are known for a unique flammable substance vital for metalworking. These dark, treacherous caverns are riddled with self-resetting traps and hazardous conditions, posing significant dangers for delvers. The air carries a distinct kerosene-like odor, indicating the presence of an oily slick along the stone walls.

The Enduring Lesser Lights discovered this slick during their descent into the crevice, recognizing they were in a fuel-laden tunnel. As they ventured deeper, oppressive conditions prevailed, and signs of previous delvers disappeared, highlighting the imminent dangers. They encountered a hulking goblinoid creature altered by the Umbra, leading to a fierce confrontation. The ichor from the brute ignited the slick on the walls, triggering a chain reaction that engulfed the area in flames. The chaotic battle revealed the caverns' intricate and dangerous nature, as The Enduring Lesser Lights faced ambushes from goblins and observed the systematic fortification of the corridors.

The environment actively influenced the challenges encountered as they navigated the perilous terrain and confronted lurking threats, further emphasizing Salthewn's significance as a backdrop for the rogue's motivations and alliances. The city's impressive skyline is visible from the surrounding mountainous areas, guiding travelers towards their destination, while the ongoing threat of gremlins damaging wooden barricades presents additional challenges for its residents.

Recent discoveries have revealed a connection between Salthewn and the cavern known as Salthewn, located at the edge of a Moon Rune Carving leading into the World Below. This cavern features a towering structure inhabited by automaton-like creatures, with an entrance surrounded by a jelly or lichen-like mob that constricts movement. The climate within Salthewn is dark and potentially hazardous, home to various creatures, including large flying mantis-like beings that exhibit a phasing property upon death.

Explorers noted that massive creatures had partially phased into stone after death, with only parts of those that died within solid matter retaining their phasing quality, which could be useful for crafting. A witch named Sylvie surveyed the World Below through Moon Rune Carving, revealing a barren landscape with titans engaged in construction. During their exploration, the party encountered Valghast, a winged lion who inquired about their allegiance to his mistress. After Valghast departed to fetch her, Ygva, a powerful witch, arrived riding him and expressed her intention to collect god remnants to prevent the upper world’s ruin. She planned to construct a massive pyre within Salthewn to draw the gods back into the World Below, offering quests to retrieve god-touched artifacts from dangerous locations, including the Thistleglass forest, typically avoided by Salthewn's inhabitants due to its unpredictable fairy denizens. The location serves as a central point for the gathering of divine remnants and the potential restoration of balance between the worlds.
