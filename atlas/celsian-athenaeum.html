Celsian Athenaeum

The Celsian Athenaeum is a prestigious college and library located in the far west, known as an ancient repository of knowledge and artifacts. It serves as a center for education, attracting scholars and students who seek to explore its extensive resources on history, magic, and ancient civilizations. Vast halls filled with preserved treasures and remnants of divine significance create an academic atmosphere where scholar-like academics share their insights, enhancing the understanding of the world's lore. The Athenaeum is dedicated to the preservation of knowledge and artifacts, emphasizing a philosophy of safeguarding wisdom rather than exploiting it for power. This ethos is exemplified by the expulsion of Ygva, a former initiate who practiced "depletion," a technique that drains the essence of entities to enhance magical effects.

Significant to the party's journey is Tamber, a tutor connected to their quest and a Celsian Itinerant. He has a history with Ygva, having trained together at the Athenaeum, which underscores the tension between her destructive practices and the Athenaeum's preservationist ethos. Locals recognize the Athenaeum's importance in understanding regional history and ancient symbols believed to date back to the age of titans, marking it as a notable point of interest. The influence of the Celsian Athenaeum is apparent as the party navigates the ongoing crisis involving Ygva and the Askavir, while Tamber retains knowledge and techniques that Ygva did not learn, indicating a potential advantage in future encounters.

Recent developments indicate that Ygva intends to raid the Athenaeum, seeking god-touched artifacts that are crucial for her objectives. Her plans highlight a growing tension, as she perceives the Athenaeum not merely as a repository but as a potential source for gathering powerful treasures linked to the gods. The implications of her actions position the Celsian Athenaeum as a critical site for both preservation and potential conflict, with treasures meant for observation now at risk of being exploited for more nefarious purposes.
