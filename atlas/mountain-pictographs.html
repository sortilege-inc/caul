Mountain Pictographs

The Mountain Pictographs are a series of large, ancient carvings embedded in the mountainside, standing 20 to 30 feet tall and approximately two-thirds as wide. They are arranged in sequences that connect to small stones found at a nearby witch's camp, indicating a historical or cultural significance. The environment is characterized by rugged terrain and varying elevations, with these monumental carvings occurring every mile along the upper road. The region features a harsh climate, near the snowline, and poses hazards such as avalanches.

The area is marked by interactions with the pictographs, which are noted for their similarity to the previously discovered stones at the witch's camp. A campsite near one of the pictographs provides shelter in the deep grooves of the rock. Among the carvings, a moon rune is observed, although no cave or entrance is present. The peculiar behavior of a compass during exploration suggests possible magical influences. The ancient landscape is navigated with care as the group considers their next moves.
